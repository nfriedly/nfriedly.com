Work

And whatsoever ye do, do it heartily, as to the Lord, and not unto men;

Colossians 3:23

I like to work. Most of my work is playing with computers, but I can enjoy sweeping a floor. I laid tile for a couple of years.

Even though I went to school for it, I am mostly self-taught when it comes to computers and web development. I can certainly thank iBoomerang for giving me the opportunity to grow and sharpen my skills. They were a great employer and I would recommend them to anyone looking for online marketing.

I did freelance web development for a number of years and worked with an amazing batch of fantastic companies (Pop Design Works was my absolute favorite). After that I joined a small startup named Rondavu which later grew into Sociable Labs.

After that, I worked on the Watson team at IBM doing all sorts of cool stuff with JavaScript and robotics and AI, then at Tanium, helping the US goverment and many large companies secure and manage their computers.

I'm now back to the old freelancing gig, although it's evolved a bit and grown a proper identity of it's own: ◢ Incline
